COVID-19 vaccine during pregnancy?

  • Dr. Darren Elder
  • 22 Aug 2021
  • 12 Comments
  • Health Tips

Healthcare experts believe that during pregnancy, women are more likely to experience severe COVID-19 infections than non-pregnant women which in turn increases the risk of serious pregnancy outcomes in them, such as preterm birth.
Records show that there are no harmful effects on women who choose to take the COVID-19 vaccine. On the same side, when we are looking at the transfer of immunity to their babies, some records show those antibodies do get transferred across the placenta to the baby.

Healthcare experts believe COVID-19 vaccine is completely safe to breastfeeding women. No doubt, antibodies are detected in breast milk, but it is still a question that how well those antibodies get absorbed by the baby, and if they end up being protective or not.

We see a lot of pregnant women get confused with the decision of taking their vaccine dose, and we like to encourage them to first consult a healthcare specialist. The one who takes care of them in their pregnancy and can advise them when and how to get vaccinated…!
